The Basics of Shooting with Film

Getting Started

When using an analog camera, the controls can seem daughting at first but once you gain a basic understanding you will become a pro in no time. There are some key terms you will need to know before getting set up.

Essential Terms

Film
A thin flexible strip of plastic or other material coated with light-sensitive emulsion for exposure in a camera, used to produce photographs or motion pictures.
ISO/ASA
The number related to the light sensitivity of a roll of film.
Shutter Speed
The speed at which the shutter of the camera closes when taking a photo.

Things you will need

  1. A Camera (preferably with a strap)
  2. A roll or cartrage of film
  3. Batteries, for light meter (if you have one)
  4. A Local Film Lab to Develop photos (or do it yourself!)

Choose a Roll of Film

The format of your fill will depend on the type of camera you will use. Yet, understanding what lighting, effects, and colors you want your photos be in will depend on the film you choose.

*pro tip: keep your film stored in a dry, dark and cold place like a fridge! This can improve the shelf life of the chemicals within the film.


How to Shoot

Once you have selected your film and loaded it into the camera it's now time to shoot! You may go through a few rolls before getting the hang of the maunal controls. It can be helpful to bring a notebook along with you and write down the settings used for each roll or frame. This can help build a frame a refrance for the effects of ISO, Shutter, Spead and Enviormental Lighting.
